# Group Contract - Team 21

## Purpose, Objectives, and Values

The purpose of this team is to learn effective software development practices while building meaningful software together. We aim to create a collaborative, high-performing environment where all members contribute and grow.

Our core values:

- Respect all team members — no role or contribution is more important than another  
- Maintain clear, consistent communication  
- Support each other’s growth and development  
- Treat mistakes as learning opportunities, not failures  
- Prioritize both strong outcomes and a positive team experience  

---

## Communication Rules

- Slack will be the primary platform for all communication  
- Important decisions must be communicated publicly in Slack  
- Use @mentions when addressing specific individuals  
- All members are expected to respond within **24 hours**  

---

## Weekly Practices

### Standup Meetings
- The team will meet weekly to discuss:
  - Progress from the previous week  
  - Goals for the upcoming week  
  - Any blockers or issues  
- Members must attend synchronously or communicate in advance if unavailable  
- Active participation (speaking or chat) is expected  

### Weekly Updates
Each member must post a weekly update in Slack including:
- What they completed  
- What they are working on next  
- Any blockers or challenges  

---

## Team Structure

Instead of fixed individual roles, the team will operate through collaborative sub-teams.

Each member is expected to join **at least 2 teams**, and may join more if desired.

### Development Teams

- **Frontend Team 1**
- **Frontend Team 2**
- **Backend Team 1**
- **Backend Team 2**
- **Design Team 1**
- **Design Team 2**

For each major feature or implementation:
- One team will be responsible for **implementation**
- The paired team will be responsible for **reviewing, testing, and validating**

This ensures:
- Code quality  
- Shared understanding across the team  
- Reduced risk of errors  

---

### Specialized Teams

#### UX/UI Team
- Responsible for proposing design concepts and user experience ideas  
- Will present ideas to the full team for feedback and alignment  

#### System Design Team
- Responsible for overall system architecture and design decisions  
- Focuses on high-level structure rather than direct implementation  

---

## My Assignments

I will contribute mainly to Frontend Team 1 and Backend Team 1. I will complete assigned tasks on time, communicate in Slack, and support my teammates through collaboration and code review.

---

## Task Management

### Definition of Done

A task is considered complete only when:

- Code is pushed to GitHub  
- A pull request is created  
- At least one team member reviews it  
- Basic testing is completed (no obvious bugs)  

---

### Workflow Expectations

- Tasks should be broken into **small, manageable pieces**  
- Work should be done **in parallel whenever possible**  
- Large features must be divided across teams when appropriate  
- If a member is blocked, they should communicate early  

---

## Coding Practices

- Any tools or frameworks may be used if documented  
- All code must be tested before submission  
- Pull requests should include:
  - Clear description of changes  
  - Any relevant context for reviewers  

---

## Asking for Help

- Team members are encouraged to ask questions early rather than working in isolation  
- Slack should be the first place to ask for help  
- Office hours (professor/TA/tutor) are strongly encouraged when needed  

---

## Logistics and Coordination

- Most logistical responsibilities (meeting setup, coordination, reminders) will be handled by **Aaran and Andy**  
- All team members are encouraged to step up and assist when needed  
- Initiative and proactive contributions are valued  

---

## Conflict Resolution

If conflicts arise:

1. Attempt to resolve directly between involved members  
2. Bring the issue to the team for discussion if needed  
3. If unresolved, escalate to team leads  
4. If necessary, involve instructional staff  

All discussions should remain respectful and professional  

---

## Non-Compliance

- Consistent lack of participation or communication may be escalated to instructional staff  
- Exceptions will be made for legitimate circumstances if communicated
